Recipe for Indian Chicken Curry (Murgh Curry)

Ingredients Needed

  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 1 and 1/2 cups chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 (15-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (divided)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

About Recipe

Prep Time Cook Time Total Time Servings
20 minutes 40 minutes 1 hour 6 servings

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Sprinkle the chicken with 2 teaspoons of salt.
Step 2: Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. Partially cook the chicken in batches until browned on all sides. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
Step 3: Reduce heat to medium. Add chopped onion, garlic, and ginger to the skillet. Cook for 5–8 minutes until the onion is soft and translucent.
Step 4: Stir in curry powder, cumin, turmeric, coriander, cayenne, and 1 tablespoon water. Cook for 1 minute while stirring.
Step 5: Add crushed tomatoes, yogurt, 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro, and 1 teaspoon salt. Stir to combine.
Step 6: Return the chicken to the skillet with its juices. Add 1/2 cup water. Bring to a boil and coat the chicken with sauce. Sprinkle with garam masala and remaining cilantro.
Step 7: Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked (internal temperature: 165°F / 74°C).
Step 8: Drizzle with lemon juice before serving.
